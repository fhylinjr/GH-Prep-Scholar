FINANCIAL AID PROCESS

As a prospective Ghanaian international student, if you intend to apply for need-based aid, you need to gather some documents and fill some forms for the colleges to determine your financial need. For each document in the important docs section, we will explain what it is and how to access it. It is advisable to start gathering documents with your parents early as schools have financial aid application deadlines.
We have also included two videos explaining the entire financial stage in detail as well. It includes, each of the documents a typical Ghanaian student will need, how to get htem, what and how to fill the entire CSS profile, what and how to fill and submit the entire ISFAA (if needed), what and how to fill the entire Certification of Finances (COF - if requested), what and how to upload required docs to IDOCs (if requested)

Additional Notes

Schools will require you to fill out the CSS Profile on College Board and pay for them to sent to colleges. College board charges $16 to send it to each shcool and you can bypass this fee by emailing the admissions/financial office of each school for a waiver code indicating the burden it presents to your family.
Some schools have limited waivers so they may request you to fill the International Student Financial Aid Application (ISFAA) which is also available on college board for international students and email it to them or upload on your college-specific portal.
NOTE: There are a few schools that will still require you to pay CSS and will neither offer waiver code nor accept ISFAA like Vassar so be mindful of that and always check the schools site.

Important Documents

Parent's Tax Returns

This is basically a document showing all sources of income your parent(s) received for a particular year as well as all taxes and deductions accrued as well. It also includes assets your parents may own. Your parents can request a copy at your local Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) office.
Most schools will just ask for the previous year's tax returns but if possible try and get the tax returns for the previous 2 years

Parent's Bank Statements/Payslips

Bank statements show how much your parents have in their accounts and also shows their expenditure pattern It can be easily obtained at their local bank branch.
The Payslips show what is the monthly income of your parents from their company. Your parents can obtain their payslips at their company's payroll/HR department

All Other Expenses

It will be useful for you and your parents to sit together and take account of all other expenditures within the previous tax year such as educational, medical , utilities, transportation, food, clothing, entertainment, etc
This is useful for filling financial aid forms such as CSS Profile, ISFAA and COF. Always make sure those expenditures can be accounted for by your parents income and adds up correctly.

CSS Profile/ ISFAA

The CSS Profile is provided by College Board and you fill it with information on the financial documents gathered as well as personal and family information to allow the colleges to get a sense of your financial situation to correctly determine your need as well as Expected Family Contribution. You fill it by signing up on college board (same site you register for SAT with)
The ISFAA is similar to the CSS Profile but provided for international students who might struggle to pay for the CSS Profile to be submitted to schools and/or do not have CSS Profile waiver codes.
Visit the CSS Profile site to learn more and begin the process.
