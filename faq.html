FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS/ CONTACT US

Thank you for your support so far on our channels and on here. So far we have compiled a list of some questions received through contacts and meetings with prospective applicants. Feel free to scroll through them. If you still do not find your question answered, shoot us an email or reach out at any of our social media handles.

  • What if my parents do not have tax returns or are self employed or one is deceased?
    • In that case you have to select the option: "Not filed, and not required to file, a tax return" under the question: "Select the student's parents' federal tax return status for (year)"
  • Can I choose not to write the SAT?
    • For now most schools are test-optional meaning you can choose not to write it. However, it is strongly recommended to at least to write it and only choose not to submit it if you do not perform well because SAT is a very strong data point that can be helpful. However if it is impossible for you to write it, that is okay as well. Just keep working on all other aspects of your application and make sure they are strong as well.
  • Does the application process here apply to universities in other countries?
    • This process is really tailored to most US universities however, after going through this process, there are are lots of similarities for which you can apply to other universities in other countries. If other universities are asking for information for which you have no idea how to proceed, you can ask them for help (they are always willing to help clarify) or you can reach out to us at ghprepscholar@gmail.com
  • Between SAT and A-Levels which is more important?
    • We would not necessarily compare both because A-Levels is usually a national leaving exam while the SAT is just an aptitude exam set by College Board to those willing to study in most US colleges. Regardless, A-Levels is a highly recognized exam compared to other national leaving exams but not every high school offers that so that is why it is important to look at the options you have. It is always advisable to write the SAT/ACT if you can.
  • What is the difference between need based and merit based aid?
    • As the name suggests, need-based aid is simply based on your family's financial inability to pay for your total educational expenses and that is usually determined based on financial information (income, taxes, assets) you provide for the college to calculate that need. The more money your family earns, the less your need based aid might be. Merit based aid is usually based on some accomplishments. Maybe you had a very high SAT score or valedictorian or you wrote a scholarship essay or you were very involved with your community. Basically, you could get merit-based aid whether your family is a high-income or low-income one. Your financial aid package might be either one or a combination of both.
  • What if I have an offer from an early decision school and a regular decision school but i prefer to go to the regular decision school?
    • Early Decision is binding however the most secure way to step out of that contract is if the early decision school gave you financial aid lower than what the regular decision school gave you and because you need to have your needs met fully to attend you can use that as a reason to step out of your contract. If not it is quite risky to step out of that binding commitment.
  • Would not getting an interview hamper my chances of getting in?
    • For some schools interviews are randomized so you may or may not get invited which is totally fine. However if you do get selected for an interview, it is strongly recommended you accept and attend it. Some also provide options earlier on to apply for an interview and I would say that is optional. Most students get in without an interview. It really can help when you know a lot about the school and the program you are vying for.
  • Can Ghanaian International students get SAT fee waivers?
    • In our experience, we have never seen or heard any Ghanaian secure one so unfortunately you will have to pay to take the test.
  • In case I get an offer and the financial aid is not enough what do I do?
    • First of all congrats on securing an offer. Not getting enough aid is not the last straw yet. You can appeal for an upward review of the financial aid and it has worked for multiple students before. You just have to send an email to the Financial Aid Office explaining your situation and usually they might review it. They might also be able to provide you with on-campus employment/ loan options you could explore. You never know till you try.
  • Do I have to write TOEFL/ IELTS?
    • No you do not. Fortunately english is the medium of instruction for all high schools in Ghana and is the official language of the country hence you can indicate that to the school if requested.
